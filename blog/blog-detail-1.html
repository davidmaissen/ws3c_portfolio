Miro - or how to digitize post-its ...

Tags: Miro Collaboration
In the last few weeks, many of us have become pros at using online meetings. Those who are not yet, now have the chance to remedy the situation with the online whiteboard Miro and finally organize their creative thoughts again in the form of Post-its and Co.
At first glance, Miro appears so versatile that it is difficult to describe the possibilities in a few words. The description "Google Docs for meetings and workshops" is probably the most appropriate. The online whiteboard can be used to record ideas, develop concepts and exchange information within the team. The application options range from simple meetings to extensive workshops - all with real-time editing by the entire team.

Boards as a playground for teams

The basic framework of Miro are the so-called boards, on which the actual collaboration takes place and which can be managed in a clear dashboard. A board - in other words, a huge digital surface - can be created with just a few clicks and then designed or even defaced according to your own wishes. To prevent the latter from happening, Miro provides a variety of templates that map well-known frameworks like Kanban or creativity methods like brainwriting. If all this is not enough, you can also define your own templates and share them with the team.

Order through frames

If you want to create order on the board, you can divide the area with frames and thus separate individual topics or focal points from one another. Frames also offer the advantage that they can be exported in various file formats such as JPG or PDF. Frames can be used to delimit and structure individual topics. Any number of frames and templates can be placed on a board. Packed toolbox The user interface of Miro appears well structured and tidy. Nevertheless, numerous tools are available that are intuitive to use. The text tool can be used to compose or insert texts. A freehand tool literally cries out for creative input from teammates. A classic can be found with the so-called sticky notes, which also deserve the name in this case. Like their analog counterparts, the notes can be enriched with information and attached - with the difference that the digital post-its always stick. If you want to go one step further, you can create complex charts and use tags to create additional structures. Videos, web pages and PDFs can also be embedded with ease.

Tasks always in control

With Cards, tasks can be added to the board, given a date and assigned to the appropriate team member. Working with Cards offers the advantage that they can be viewed like frames in a detailed navigation and filtered by the respective user.

Taking collaboration and cooperation to a new level

The view of the board alone is fascinating, as the various team members buzz around the workspace like busy bees and the board takes on new forms. In addition to this real-time editing, simple comments, a text and video chat, sreensharing and, last but not least, the possibility for voting are available. To prevent team members from getting lost in the board and forgetting the time, a timer can also be set. Of course, a board can also be shared with external circles via a link.

Tuning by integrations and apps

Those who use tools like Slack, Microsoft Teams or Google Drive can activate a variety of apps in Miro's Marketplace to connect the different tools. In addition to the browser version, Miro also offers desktop apps for macOS and Windows, as well as apps for iOS and Android.

Free starter

In the free version, Miro offers a total of three boards for an unlimited number of users and an unlimited time period. For larger teams, a team and business version is available for $8 and $16 per month, respectively. The tool and more information can be found at miro.com.
